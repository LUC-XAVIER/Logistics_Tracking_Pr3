How Weather Affects the Price — Formula & Logic
The base cost formula from the project documentation is:

final_price = base_price × distance_factor + (fragility_level × 500)

The weather integration introduces a weather surcharge multiplier on top of this. Each adverse condition adds to the multiplier — heavy rain, strong winds, extreme cold or heat, and low visibility all increase handling risk, slow down the vehicle, and may require special packaging for fragile items.

The speed adjustment combines the existing fragility formula with a weather penalty:
adjusted_speed = base_speed × (1 − fragility/15) × (1 − weather_penalty)

A higher weather penalty means slower delivery and higher cost — this feeds directly into the ETA and the surcharge shown above.
